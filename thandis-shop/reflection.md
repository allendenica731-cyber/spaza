# Reflection

## What Was Hardest

The most challenging part of this project was not the coding itself, it was everything around it.

My laptop broke down partway through the challenge, which was incredibly frustrating and stressful. I had to borrow a laptop and get back up to speed, which cost me valuable time and focus. That alone was a significant setback.

The other major hurdle was deployment. After building the app, I kept hitting a "page not found" error after deploying and I could not figure out why. This was genuinely crushing because the app worked locally but would not serve correctly once deployed. I had to seek help by asking Claude and watching YouTube tutorials and working through the problem step by step. Eventually I learned that the issue was related to how the build output was being served and I resolved it by copying the dist folder directly to Netlify and then pushing the full React app from Visual Code to get the deployment working properly. That process taught me more about deployment than I expected.

Understanding the full project structure at the start was also confusing. Knowing where to begin, from generating the React app in Visual Code, to wiring everything together, to updating the README, required me to piece together knowledge from multiple sources.

## What I Would Do Differently With More Time

I would spend more time upfront planning the folder structure and deployment pipeline before writing any code. A lot of my frustration came from figuring out deployment at the end under pressure. Setting up a proper workflow like connecting GitHub directly to Vercel or Netlify from the beginning would have saved hours.

I would also add more features tailored specifically to Mama Thandi, things like low stock alerts, a quick sold one button for common items and basic offline support so the app works without data.

## What I Learned

This project pushed me into territory I had not worked in before. I have built websites using Gemini AI assistance through my IFS modules over the past two semesters so prompting and working with AI felt natural. But building a mobile web app, thinking about a real user like Mama Thandi, handling deployment and debugging a live environment was a completely new experience for me.

I learned that deployment is its own skill separate from coding. I also learned that hitting obstacles like a broken laptop and a broken deployment is part of the process and that being resourceful, asking for help, watching tutorials and trying different approaches is just as important as technical knowledge. I am proud that I did not give up.
